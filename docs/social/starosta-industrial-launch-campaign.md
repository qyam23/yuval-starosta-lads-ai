## Campaign Summary
The launch campaign was structured as a 4-day public introduction:
1. Industrial Intelligence
2. ER Labs
3. Automation and Control
4. Factory and Process Engineering

The campaign presented one consistent message: understand the real process first, then apply engineering, automation, data, and AI where they create measurable operational value.

## Day 1: Industrial Intelligence
The first launch post established the main public framing:
- factories often do not lack data
- the main problem is fragmented data disconnected from the real process
- before AI, dashboards, or automation, the real process must be understood
- Starosta Industrial connects industrial engineering, automation, operational data, and applied intelligence

The post also introduced the public themes that the company intends to discuss:
- Industrial Engineering
- Automation and Control
- Manufacturing Intelligence
- Factory and Process Engineering
- Applied AI in real production environments

## Day 2: ER Labs
The second launch post introduced ER Labs as a lightweight industrial intelligence layer developed under Starosta Labs.

The public message was:
- ER Labs is a lightweight industrial intelligence layer
- it connects plant data, structures signals, and turns fragmented information into operational clarity
- it is intended for real production environments rather than abstract analytics

The campaign identified the kinds of source systems and operational signals that matter in real factories:
- PLC
- SCADA
- historians
- manual reports and records
- alarms
- machine states
- operational data distributed across multiple plant systems

## Day 3: Automation and Control
The third launch post clarified the public position on automation:
- automation is not only about making machines run
- good automation makes production behavior stable, visible, diagnosable, and understandable
- control systems must support operators and real decision-making during operation

The post highlighted these focus areas:
- PLC logic structure
- HMI visibility
- alarms
- diagnostics
- line coordination
- signal traceability
- troubleshooting
- uptime

## Day 4: Factory and Process Engineering
The fourth launch post framed factory engineering as a process-centered discipline:
- a factory is a working system, not only a building with machines
- layout affects flow, operators, utilities, maintenance, safety, throughput, and future expansion
- Starosta Industrial approaches factory engineering from the process inward

The post connected factory engineering to:
- layout and material flow
- utilities and infrastructure planning
- process equipment integration
- production readiness
- maintenance and access logic
- safety and operational constraints
- future expansion planning
